Quick Sort

What is Quick Sort?

Quick Sort is a popular sorting algorithm that uses a divide-and-conquer approach to sort elements efficiently. It selects a pivot element and partitions the array into two sub-arrays according to the pivot, then recursively sorts the sub-arrays.

Example:

Consider the following array: [38, 27, 43, 3, 9, 82, 10]

Output:


        

          Applying Quick Sort:
          [3, 9, 10, 27, 38, 43, 82]
        

      

    


    

      
Complexity Analysis of Quick Sort:

      
Time Complexity: O(N log N) on average, O(N^2) in worst case

      
Auxiliary Space: O(log N) on average, O(N) in worst case

    


    

      
Advantages of Quick Sort:

      
    
        
  • Efficient for large datasets.
    • 
        
  • Optimal average-case performance.
    • 
        
  • Space complexity is relatively low.
    • 
        
  • Adaptable; performs well with partially sorted arrays.
    • 
      

    


    

      
Disadvantages of Quick Sort:

      
    
        
  • Worst-case time complexity can be O(N^2), although rare.
    • 
        
  • Not stable; may change the relative order of equal elements.
    • 
        
  • Recursive implementation may lead to stack overflow for very large datasets.
